<a>
<h5>Discount on Income Tax payable by Individuals </h5></a>
Any individuals who makes any quarterly self-assessment income tax payment, one month before the due date, is entitled to a 10% discount thereon (not exceeding such percentage on the installment based on the current year profits).
<p>
<strong>Illustration – I </strong>
<span>A</span><span>ssume tha</span><span>t your income tax liability (as an individual) for the year of assessment 2015/16 is Rs. 200,000/-. Then, under normal circumstances you may make quarterly self-assessment income tax payments for the year of assessment 2016/17, as follows:</span></p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<th>Payment Date</th>
<th>Calculation</th>
<th>Amount (Rs.)</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th August 2016</td>
<td>200,000 x ¼</td>
<td>50,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th November 2016</td>
<td>200,000 x ¼</td>
<td>50,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th February 2017</td>
<td>200,000 x ¼</td>
<td>50,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th May 2017</td>
<td>200,000 x ¼</td>
<td>50,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Total</td>
<td>200,000</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p> However, if you make above  payments one month before the respective stipulated dates, you are entitled to  a 10% discount thereof (based on current year liability) and payments could be  made as follows.</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<th>Payment Date</th>
<th>Calculation</th>
<th>Amount (Rs.)</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th July 2016</td>
<td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td>
<td>45,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th Oct 2016</td>
<td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td>
<td>45,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th January 2017</td>
<td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td>
<td>45,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th April 2017</td>
<td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td>
<td>45,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td> Total</td>
<td>180,000</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p> If your income tax liability for the year of assessment 2016/17  is Rs. 250,000/-,
then, the balance tax payable for that year of assessment, on or before  September 30, 2017, is as follows:</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<th>Computation</th>
<th>Amount (Rs.)</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Total Income Tax payable for the Y/A 2016/17</td>
<td>250,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Less: Total of Quarterly self-assessment tax payments made</td>
<td>(180,000)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Discount 200,000 x 10% (already deducted)</td>
<td>(20,000)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<strong>Balance Tax payable on or before September 30, 2017</strong></td>
<td>
<strong>50,000</strong></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>
</p>

<p>Assume that your income tax liability (as an individual) for the  year of assessment 2015/16 is Rs.200,000/-.
Then, under normal circumstances you may make quarterly self-assessment income  tax payments on the basis of that liability for the year of assessment 2016/17  as follows:</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<th>Payment Date</th>
<th>Calculation</th>
<th>Amount (Rs.)</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th August 2016</td>
<td>200,000 x ¼</td>
<td>50,000</td>
</tr>

<td>200,000 X ¼</td>
<td> 50,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th February 2017</td>
<td>200,000 X ¼​</td>
<td> 50,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th May 2017</td>
<td>200,000 X ¼</td>
<td> 50,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<strong>Total</strong></td>
<td>
<strong>200,000</strong></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>However, if you make above payments one month before respective  stipulated dates, you will be entitled to a 10% discount thereof (based on  current year liability) and payments could be made as follows.</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<th>Payment Date</th>
<th>Calculation</th>
<th>Amount (Rs.)</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th July 2016</td>
<td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td>
<td>45,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th Oct 2016</td>
<td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td>
<td>45,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th January 2017</td>
<td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td>
<td>45,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>15th April 2017</td>
<td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td>
<td>45,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<strong>Total</strong></td>
<td>
<strong>180,000</strong></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>​If your income tax liability for the year of assessment 2016/17  is Rs. 150,000/-,         then the refund for that year of assessment, is computed as follows.</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<th>Computation</th>
<th>Amount (Rs.)</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Total Income Tax payable for the Y/A 2016/17</td>
<td>150,000</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Less: Total of Quarterly self-assessment tax payments</td>
<td>(180,000)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td> Discount 150,000 x 10% (entitled)</td>
<td>(15,000)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<strong>Refund due for the Y/A 2016/17</strong></td>
<td>
<strong>(45,000)</strong></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>
<span>Please note that the discount is limited to 10% of the current year tax liability.</span><span>​​​</span></p>

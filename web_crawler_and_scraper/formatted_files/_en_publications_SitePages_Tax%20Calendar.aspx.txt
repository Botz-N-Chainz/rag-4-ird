Any individuals who makes any quarterly self-assessment income tax payment, one month before the due date, is entitled to a 10% discount thereon (not exceeding such percentage on the installment based on the current year profits).
<p><strong>Illustration – I </strong><span>A</span><span>ssume tha</span><span>t your income tax liability (as an individual) for the year of assessment 2015/16 is Rs. 200,000/-. Then, under normal circumstances you may make quarterly self-assessment income tax payments for the year of assessment 2016/17, as follows:</span></p><table><tbody><tr><th>Payment Date</th><th>Calculation</th><th>Amount (Rs.)</th></tr><tr><td>15th August 2016</td><td>200,000 x ¼</td><td>50,000</td></tr><tr><td>15th November 2016</td><td>200,000 x ¼</td><td>50,000</td></tr><tr><td>15th February 2017</td><td>200,000 x ¼</td><td>50,000</td></tr><tr><td>15th May 2017</td><td>200,000 x ¼</td><td>50,000</td></tr><tr><td>Total</td><td>200,000</td></tr></tbody></table><p> However, if you make above  payments one month before the respective stipulated dates, you are entitled to  a 10% discount thereof (based on current year liability) and payments could be  made as follows.</p><table><tbody><tr><th>Payment Date</th><th>Calculation</th><th>Amount (Rs.)</th></tr><tr><td>15th July 2016</td><td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td><td>45,000</td></tr><tr><td>15th Oct 2016</td><td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td><td>45,000</td></tr><tr><td>15th January 2017</td><td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td><td>45,000</td></tr><tr><td>15th April 2017</td><td>(200,000 x ¼) less 200,000x ¼ x10%</td><td>45,000</td></tr><tr><td> Total</td><td>180,000</td></tr></tbody></table><p> If your income tax liability for the year of assessment 2016/17  is Rs. 250,000/-,
